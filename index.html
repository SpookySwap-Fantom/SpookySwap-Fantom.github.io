Architecture & Execution on Fantom

SpookySwap executes locally on Fantom, keeping gas low and confirmation latency short. The router searches stable (stables/pegged or strongly correlated pairs) and volatile pools (constant product) and chooses single-hop or multi-hop paths such as TOKEN ‚Üí FTM ‚Üí TOKEN when intermediate liquidity is deeper.

Pool Types & When to Use Them

  • Stable Pools: Best for correlated assets (e.g., USDC/USDT). Curve-like curve reduces slippage around the peg.
  • Volatile Pools (x*y=k): For non-correlated assets (e.g., FTM/ALT). Expect higher slippage for large orders.
  • Composite Paths: Router may bridge through a deep intermediary (often WFTM) to improve effective price.

Route Quality = Output ‚àí (Fees + Gas + Slippage)

  • AMM Fees: Embedded in pool math; vary by pool tier.
  • Gas on Fantom: Typically low; multi-hop still adds cost, so the router weighs net outcome.
  • Price Impact: Depth matters; splitting size or using a stable hop can help.

Why Local Execution Matters

No cross-chain latency or bridge risks for swaps. Funding only the FTM gas token is enough for approvals and settlement.

Operational Safety & Threat Mitigation

SpookySwap is non-custodial: assets move directly between your wallet and AMM pools. You still control approvals and sign every transaction. Adopt these practices to reduce risk:

Wallet & Allowance Hygiene

  • Use a hardware wallet for material balances; verify token, amount, and recipient on device.
  • Approve minimal allowances (not unlimited) and periodically revoke stale permissions.
  • Bookmark official links; avoid interacting with unsolicited ‚Äúsupport.‚Äù

MEV Awareness on Fantom

  • Set tight slippage on liquid pairs; widen only when depth is thin.
  • Prefer private/builder RPC if your wallet supports it; large orders ‚Üí consider splitting.
  • Submit during calmer volatility to reduce inclusion delays and sandwich exposure.

Sanity Checks Before Signing

  • Confirm token contracts on FTMScan.
  • Inspect tx prompts: router target, data size, estimated gas, and the pool path shown in UI.
  • Avoid ‚Äútransfer-fee/tax‚Äù tokens unless you understand their behavior (they can cause route failures or misquotes).
